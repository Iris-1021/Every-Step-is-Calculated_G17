Why York?

York is a historic city in North Yorkshire, UK, renowned for its medieval charm, thriving tourism, and vibrant cultural life. However, like many modern cities, York also faces growing challenges around urban safety—especially after dark.

In 2023, the crime rate here reached 96 per thousand people, which is 14% higher than the average level of the entire North Yorkshire county. It ranks 31st among 699 cities, towns, and villages in the UK.

At night in York, danger is not only hidden in the shadows. Violence and sexual crimes are the most common types of crimes, with over 5200 cases reported in 2023 alone, accounting for almost one-third of the total. Anti-social behavior crimes reached its peak in January 2024, with 204 incidents occurring in just one month, second to none in North Yorkshire.

So we choose York not only because it is "dangerous", but also because it is real and exposes the safety issues faced by women in modern cities. Their night route is worth seeing - and our project starts from here.

Why we focus on women?

In the year ending March 2023, the proportion of women experiencing sexual assault, stalking, and harassment is significantly higher than that of men (Dodd, 2024).

The first national report released by the National Police Chief Commission in the UK states that approximately 2 million women become victims of male violence each year, involving various behaviors such as stalking, harassment, and sexual assault (ONS, 2024). These crimes affect approximately 1 in every 12 women in England and Wales, and related criminal records have increased by 37% in the past five years.

These shocking data profoundly reveal the ongoing security challenges faced by women, so we choose to focus our research on the female perspective.

Details

Left Map — Single Safety Factor View

  • Displays street safety indices for individual factors, including Crime, Bus, Nightlife places, Lighting, and CCTV.
  • Use the dropdown menu to select which factor appears as the top layer.
  • Adjust the opacity sliders to control the transparency of each layer and explore how different factors influence various areas.
  • The radar chart shows the weights and positive or negative impact of each safety factor.

Right Map — Integrated Safety Score View

  • Combines all safety factors and their weights to calculate an overall street safety score.
  • The gradient colors help you easily identify areas with different safety levels for comparison.

Center Slider

  • Drag left or right to compare the single-factor map with the integrated safety score map.
  • This helps you better understand how individual factors and the combined safety score relate to each other.

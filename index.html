Why York?

York is a historic city in North Yorkshire, UK, renowned for its medieval charm,and cultural vibrancy. With growing tourist numbers, the city also exhibits a highly active night-time urban dynamic.

In 2023, the crime rate here reached 96 per thousand people, which is 14% above the North Yorkshire averge. York ranks 31st out of 699 areas across the UK.

At night in York, danger is not exposed in the shadows. Violence and sexual crimes were the most reported crimes, with over 5200 cases in 2023, accounting for almost one-third of the total. Anti-social behavior crimes peaked in January 2024, with 204 incidents occurring in just one month, second to none in North Yorkshire.

So we choose York not only because it is "dangerous", but also because it reveals what women face at night in modern cities. Their night route is worth seeing - and our project starts from here.

Why we focus on women?

In the year ending March 2023, the proportion of women experiencing sexual assault, stalking, and harassment is significantly higher than that of men (Dodd, 2024).

The first national report released by the National Police Chief Commission in the UK states that approximately 2 million women become victims of male violence each year, involving various behaviors such as stalking, harassment, and sexual assault (ONS, 2024). These crimes affect approximately 1 in every 12 women in England and Wales, and related criminal records have increased by 37% in the past five years.

These shocking data profoundly reveal the ongoing security challenges faced by women, so we choose to focus our research on the female perspective.
